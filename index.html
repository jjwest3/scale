What I'm building

IonShield — operational space weather intelligence. The translation layer between raw NOAA/GloTEC/NASA data and the decision a dispatcher, SOF team, or satellite operator has to make in the next 90 seconds. "Will my GPS be reliable on this grid tonight? Which HF frequencies will work on this route? Should I delay this mission three hours?"

Solar Cycle 25 is at maximum right now — the May 2024 G5 was the strongest storm in 20 years and ran 40% above predictions. The DoW publishes 35,000+ space weather products a day. Zero of them are unclassified, ATAK-native, or self-service at the tactical edge. That's the gap.

What's actually shipping today

This isn't a deck. It's a production-grade platform I built solo in under four months using AI-assisted tooling (Cursor, Claude Code, Windsurf) — me driving science, product, and operator conversations; the LLMs handling implementation under tight review.

  • 324-cell global risk grid (10°lat × 20°lon), updated every 5 minutes from NOAA SWPC, GloTEC TEC, NASA OMNI HAPI, and GFZ Potsdam.
  • Physics-to-operational translation: GPS error in meters, HF reliability in dB (ITU-R P.533), L-band SATCOM fade margin — not raw Kp.
  • Live ATAK integration: KML network link, GeoJSON overlay, CoT pull feed (MIL-STD-2525B), async CoT push to TAK Server on port 8087. Zero competing ionospheric overlays exist in the 250K-device ATAK installed base.
  • Self-retraining ML pipeline with champion/challenger A/B, drift-triggered retraining, and human-readable JSON model weights — designed deliberately for DoD reviewer audit. Black-box ML doesn't get a FedRAMP ATO.
  • Palantir Foundry integration live: 4 datasets syncing every 5 minutes, ontology objects defined, Workshop-ready.
  • Three concurrent API versions, 413 tests passing, async SQLAlchemy, structured migrations, Render + Foundry dual deployment.
  • 8 historical storm replays (May 2024 G5, Halloween 2003, St. Patrick's 2015) with hour-by-hour scrubber and KMZ/CSV export.

Why this is the right team

Scale's stated mission is reliable AI for the world's most important decisions, and the hackathon job page calls out Army and Air Force partnerships specifically. IonShield's whole design thesis — auditable models, physics-grounded outputs, defense-delivery-layer-first — is the same thesis. The DoW spent 20 years and several prototypes trying to build the operator-facing layer between scientific indices and tactical decisions. The conditions to actually ship it (ATAK ubiquity, Foundry marketplace, modern airgapped delivery via Platform One/UDS) only converged in the last 18 months. That's the work I want to be doing for the next decade, and Scale is the largest team in the world doing a version of it.
